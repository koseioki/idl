## Who is affected by geographical location?

Certain groups face disadvantages in the digital society and in accessing ICT due to where they live. These include:

- Rural areas
- Remote communities
- The Global South

## Challenges

People living in rural or remote locations often face more challenges than those in urban and densely populated areas. These challenges include:

- Limited access to services and digital networks
- High costs of mobile and internet services
- Lower levels of digital literacy and motivation
- Lack of contextually and culturally appropriate solutions

Many of these challenges are linked to factors such as aging populations, geographical isolation, and lower income levels in rural areas.

This issue is commonly referred to as the “digital divide.”
